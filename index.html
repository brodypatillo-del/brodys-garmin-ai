⚠️ Supportive PT routine — not medical advice. If the twinge worsens or appears at rest, see a sports medicine doctor.
Daily Protocol · ~12 min
1. Neural Hamstring Glide
3 × 10 each leg · Daily
Priority

Sit at edge of chair, straighten one leg while flexing foot upward. Hold 2 sec, lower slowly. Mobilizes the sciatic nerve and posterior knee capsule.

⚡ No bouncing — stretch sensation only.
2. Eccentric Calf Raises
3 × 15 · Both up, single leg down · Daily
Key

Rise on two feet, lower on one over 4 seconds. Addresses gastrocnemius pull on posterior knee — a primary cause of popliteal twinges in runners.

3. Prone Banded Hamstring Curl
3 × 12 · Light band · Daily

Lie face down, curl heel toward glute slowly. Controls hamstring tendon load at the posterior knee insertion point.

4. Hip Hinge Pattern Reset
2 × 10 · Bodyweight · Daily

Hands on hips, push hips back (not down). Retrains posterior chain to absorb load through the hip, reducing knee demand during runs and squats.

5. Popliteus Stretch (Figure-4)
3 × 30 sec each leg · Post-run · #1
Post-run

Lie on back, cross one ankle over opposite knee, pull uncrossed leg toward chest. Directly stretches the popliteus muscle behind the knee.

⚡ After every single run, no exceptions.
6. Dynamic Leg Swings
20 reps each direction · Pre-run

Hold a wall, swing leg forward/back then side/side. Lubricates the knee and warms up hamstrings before impact work.

7. Foam Roll: Calves + Hamstrings
60 sec per group · Post-run or evening

Slow rolling, pausing on tender spots. Ankle to behind-knee for calves, sit bone to knee for hamstrings.

🟢

Keep training

Mild twinge that fades in warmup. Stiffness first 5 min then clears.

🔴

See a doctor

Sharp pain, swelling, pain at rest, or worsens mid-run.
